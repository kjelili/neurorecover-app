# NeuroRecover Therapist Workflow

This guide is for clinicians, physiotherapists, and caregivers supervising rehabilitation sessions with NeuroRecover.

## Clinical intent

NeuroRecover supports repetitive, task-specific upper-limb and hand rehabilitation with camera-based feedback. It is useful for monitoring trends in motor control, but it does not replace formal clinical assessment.

## Session setup checklist

- Confirm patient identity and affected side.
- Position patient safely with trunk and shoulder supported.
- Ensure camera has clear visibility of the active hand.
- Verify lighting is even and the background is not visually noisy.
- Confirm no pain flare or contraindications before starting.

## Recommended weekly cadence

- Frequency: 3 to 5 sessions per week
- Session duration: 10 to 20 minutes total
- Exercise split: rotate across all three games for balanced stimulus
- Rest intervals: 30 to 90 seconds between sets or as tolerated

Adjust intensity based on fatigue, tone, pain, and therapist judgment.

## Suggested single-session structure

1. Warm-up (1 to 2 minutes): gentle active range and wrist/finger mobility.
2. Virtual Piano (3 to 5 minutes): finger isolation and selective control.
3. Bubble Pop (3 to 5 minutes): reach accuracy and coordinated movement.
4. Cup Grasp (3 to 5 minutes): power grip activation and hold tolerance.
5. Cool-down (1 minute): relaxed hand opening/closing and breathing.

## Progress interpretation (practical)

Use trend direction over multiple sessions, not a single data point.

## Range of Motion (ROM)

- Improving trend: larger extension values over time.
- Clinical note: compare against compensatory shoulder/trunk movement.

## Tremor Index

- Improving trend: lower micro-oscillation variance over sessions.
- Clinical note: interpret with medication timing and fatigue context.

## Smoothness

- Improving trend: reduced jerkiness in movement profile.
- Clinical note: prioritize quality and control over game speed.

## Reaction Time (task dependent)

- Improving trend: faster and more consistent response latency.
- Clinical note: interpret alongside cognition, attention, and cueing style.

## Red flags and stop criteria

Stop or reduce intensity if any of the following occur:

- New or increasing pain
- Dizziness, nausea, or visual discomfort
- Significant compensatory movement
- Marked fatigue with motor breakdown

Document incident details and adjust next session plan.

## Documentation workflow

After each session:

1. Review in-app session summaries and trend charts.
2. Export PDF report for patient record when needed.
3. Record brief SOAP-style notes in your EHR or clinic notes.
4. Set next-session focus (speed, control, endurance, or ROM).

## Goal progression strategy

- Week 1 to 2: prioritize movement quality and confidence.
- Week 3 to 4: increase repetitions and dual-task challenge gradually.
- Ongoing: progress one parameter at a time (duration, precision, or tempo).

Avoid progressing multiple load factors simultaneously in low-endurance patients.

## Privacy and governance notes

- By default, NeuroRecover data is saved locally in the browser.
- Clearing browser data removes local progress history.
- Exported PDFs should follow your organization data handling policy.

## Scope reminder

NeuroRecover is a rehabilitation support tool. Clinical decisions should always follow licensed professional judgment and local care protocols.
