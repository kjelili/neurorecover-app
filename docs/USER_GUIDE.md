# NeuroRecover User Guide

This guide helps patients, caregivers, and therapists use NeuroRecover safely and effectively.

## 1) Before you start

- Use a modern browser: Chrome, Edge, or Brave (latest version).
- Make sure your device has a working camera.
- Use a well-lit room so your hand is clearly visible.
- Sit with your elbow supported on a table when possible.
- Keep your hand around 30-60 cm from the camera.

## 2) Open the app

1. Open the NeuroRecover URL in your browser.
2. Click **Allow** when the browser asks for camera permission.
3. From the landing page, open the main app dashboard.

If camera permission was blocked, open browser site settings and re-enable camera access.

## 3) Dashboard overview

From the dashboard, you can:

- Start one of the three training games
- Review your latest activity
- Track weekly progress
- Open the Progress page for charts and PDF export

## 4) Exercises and goals

## Virtual Piano

- Goal: improve finger isolation and reaction speed.
- How: move or tap specific fingers as prompted.
- Tip: keep your wrist stable and move one finger at a time.

## Bubble Pop

- Goal: improve reach, pinch accuracy, and hand-eye coordination.
- How: move your hand to pop appearing bubbles.
- Tip: prioritize smooth, controlled motion over speed.

## Cup Grasp

- Goal: train grip strength and sustained grasp pattern.
- How: close your fist and hold when instructed.
- Tip: maintain steady pressure and avoid shoulder compensation.

## 5) Progress tracking

The app tracks session metrics such as:

- Range of motion (ROM)
- Tremor index
- Smoothness
- Reaction time (where applicable)

Data is shown in session history and charts. You can export reports to PDF from the Progress page.
You can also:

- Export all sessions to CSV
- Create a JSON backup
- Import JSON backup to restore data on the same or another device

## 5.1) Session check-in and notes

During active sessions you can add optional:

- Pain level (0-10)
- Fatigue level (0-10)
- Session notes

On the Progress page, you can edit therapist annotations for completed sessions.

## 5.2) Settings and accessibility

Open Settings from the app navigation to configure:

- Weekly session goal
- Daily reminder time
- Therapist mode
- High contrast mode
- Large text mode
- Reduced motion mode
- Voice coaching prompts

## 5.3) Multi-profile mode

In Settings, you can manage local patient profiles:

- Create profile
- Switch active profile
- Rename profile
- Delete profile (except the last remaining one)

Each profile keeps its own sessions, progress charts, backups, and clinical assessments locally.

When Therapist Mode is enabled, the Clinical Tools page includes a one-click therapist bundle export (JSON) for the active profile.

## 6) Data and privacy

- NeuroRecover stores data locally in your browser (`localStorage`).
- No account is required for core use.
- Clearing browser data removes saved progress.

If you want backups, export PDF reports regularly.

## 7) Safe-use recommendations

- Stop if you feel pain, dizziness, or unusual fatigue.
- Follow your therapist's session duration and intensity advice.
- Rest between sessions as needed.
- NeuroRecover supports rehabilitation but does not replace professional medical care.

## 8) Troubleshooting

## Camera is not detected

- Confirm camera works in another browser tab/app.
- Check site camera permission settings.
- Close other apps that may be locking the camera.

## Hand tracking is unstable

- Improve lighting and reduce backlight.
- Keep your full hand inside the camera frame.
- Use a plain background when possible.

## Progress data is missing

- Check if browser storage was cleared.
- Avoid private/incognito mode for regular use.

## 9) Suggested routine

- Start with 2-3 short sessions per week.
- Use all three exercises for balanced hand training.
- Review trends weekly and adjust with your therapist.
